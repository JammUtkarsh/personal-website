MacOS screen recording with in-app audio SOLVED.

In my experience, the native screen recorder of MacOS is quite simple and effective. The only drop back of it is, it does not record in-app audio. There might be other software out there that can be used but they are either paid or have complex usage or your SSD storage is running low or maybe all.

In this ‘how-to’ guide let’s solve all the above problems.

Step 1: Download the 'VB Cable' application and install it.

Step 2: After installation, open Audio MIDI Setup.

Step 3: Click on the + icon below and choose the multi-output device option. Now,

  • Check the ‘use’ box of VB cable and your default output device(external headphones in my case).
  • Uncheck drift connect from all. Doesn’t really change anything in my usage.
  • Set ‘Master Device’ to the default output device.
  • Set ‘Sample Rate’ at 44.1kHz.
  • Also, you can rename it to ‘Output: External + VB’ to have a differentiate from other audio devices.

Step 4: Alright now, click on the + icon below and choose the aggregate device option. Now,

  • Check the ‘use’ box of VB cable
    Optional: Also check your microphone(MacBook pro microphone in my case) to include your audio as well.
  • Uncheck drift connect from all. Doesn’t really change anything in my usage.
  • Set ‘Clock Source’ to the default microphone device.
  • Set ‘Sample Rate’ at 44.1kHz.
  • Also, you can rename it to ‘Input: External + VB + Mic’ to have a distinction from other audio devices.

Now all the one-time settings are set and we are ready to go.

Step 5: Change your output device to ‘Output: External + VB’ from menubar/sound.
NOTE: Before changing the output device, set the output volume beforehand.

Step 6: Open the native screen recorder with Cmd + Shift + 5. Set it to the recording option.
Then ‘Options’ tab will appear. Click and set microphone to 'Input: External + VB + Mic’

Now can record anything, from your discord video call to recording your online lecture.

Great Thanks for reading.
Wanna know how the magic happened? Stay with me.

After setting up the recording configuration the screen audio is transmitted via the VB cable. With the help of Audio MIDI Setup, both the audio from the VB cable and microphone are treated as one parallel stream of audio in the input channel. This audio is channelized to the recording's input system.
